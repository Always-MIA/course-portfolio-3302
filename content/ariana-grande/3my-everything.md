# My Everything (2014): Becoming a Pop Superstar

![My Everything Album Cover](my-everything.jpg)

## Background

### Expanding Her Sound

Following the success of her debut album *Yours Truly*, Ariana Grande began expanding her musical style and working with a wider range of high-profile producers and artists. For *My Everything*, she collaborated with industry figures like Max Martin, Zedd, Ilya, and Shellback, which helped shift her sound toward a more mainstream pop direction. The album also featured major guest artists such as [[index-tw|The Weeknd]] and Childish Gambino, showing her growing presence in the global music industry. This era marked her transition from rising star to international pop artist.

## Musical Style

- EDM
- Dance Pop
- R&B
- Pop

*My Everything* blends emotional R&B vocals with upbeat EDM and pop production. Compared to her debut, this album is more radio-friendly and energetic, while still showcasing her vocal range and emotional delivery.

## Biggest Singles

- Problem
- Break Free
- Love Me Harder
- One Last Time

These singles played a major role in Ariana’s breakout success. *Problem* and *Break Free* especially dominated charts and introduced her to a global pop audience.

## Lasting Impact

*My Everything* was the album that established Ariana Grande as a worldwide pop superstar. It showed that she could successfully transition from R&B-influenced beginnings into mainstream pop without losing her vocal identity. The commercial success of this album solidified her place in the industry and set the stage for a more mature artistic evolution in [[4dangerous-woman|Dangerous Woman]].
